Project Overview

This page provides a complete overview of the confirmed maintenance, refresh and improvement works at Flat 225, Nell Gwynn House. All scope, pricing and documentation are set out below for your reference.

Works are aimed at restoring the flat to a high lettable standard, covering decoration throughout, bathroom remediation and re-grouting, kitchen worktop and sink replacement, shower wall remediation with a new external thermostatic system, and a new dishwasher installation.

Option 6b has been confirmed — the longer worktop will be replaced to match the new black granite. A 5% goodwill discount has been applied to the confirmed final total of £10,248.60.

Longer Worktop: Option B Confirmed by Client
The client has confirmed Option B — the longer worktop run (1,850mm) will be replaced to match the new black granite worktop in Item 5. Supply, fit, cutting and sealing included.
Item 4 — Shower Remediation
Shower Wall Tiles — History of Leaks, Urgent Remediation Recommended
Tiles on the thermostatic valve wall are coming away from the substrate and were not installed correctly. To prevent further water ingress to the flat below, we recommend full remediation: strip tiles, water-tank the substrate, re-tile with white tiles, and install a new surface-mounted Grohe thermostatic shower system to eliminate the concealed valve risk point.
